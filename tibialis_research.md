# Tibialis Trainer Program — Evidence Review

## Program Being Reviewed

- **Exercise:** Tibialis Raise (tibialis trainer bar)
- **Sets:** 3
- **Reps:** 15–25 (double progression)
- **Starting weight:** 5 kg
- **Frequency:** 2–3×/week
- **Warmup:** None

---

## Sources & Credibility

### 1. Peer-Reviewed RCT — PMC / Medicine (2023)
**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10403024/
**Published in:** *Medicine* (Wolters Kluwer / LWW), indexed on PubMed/PMC
**Credibility:** Peer-reviewed randomized controlled trial in a major clinical journal.

Rehabilitation study (n=50 hemiplegia patients). Treatment group performed 300 dorsiflexion reps/day split across 3–5 sessions over 8 weeks. Tibialis anterior RMS active contraction improved by 24.17 points vs. 5.24 in control (p=.001). Confirms the muscle responds well to high-rep, high-frequency stimulation. Not directly applicable (stroke rehab) but validates that high rep ranges cause no harm and drive adaptation.

---

### 2. Peer-Reviewed RCT — PLOS One (2024)
**URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0312152
**Published in:** *PLOS One* (open-access, peer-reviewed)
**Credibility:** RCT in female athletes, 2024.

3 sessions/week for 8 weeks produced large effect sizes for ankle dorsiflexion strength and ROM. Tibialis anterior muscle activity decreased ~15% MVIC during squat descent — indicating improved efficiency. **Validates 3×/week frequency.**

---

### 3. NMES Pilot Study — MDPI Sensors (2025)
**URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/25/8/2455
**Published in:** *Sensors* (MDPI), peer-reviewed open-access
**Credibility:** Small pilot (n=17 healthy adults). Limited applicability (electrical stimulation).

4-week protocol produced significant improvements in balance and tibialis anterior strength. Supports the notion that even short training blocks drive measurable adaptation.

---

### 4. Fiber Type Research — PMC / ResearchGate
**Credibility:** Multiple peer-reviewed histology studies indexed on PubMed and ResearchGate.

The tibialis anterior is **predominantly fast-twitch (Type II)** — majority Type IIb and IId fibers, fewer Type I (slow-twitch). Fast-twitch fibers respond preferentially to moderate-to-heavier loads (6–15 reps). The 15–25 rep range targets hypertrophy via metabolic stress but is on the high end for maximizing Type II fiber recruitment. Long-term programming should include heavier, lower-rep work.

---

### 5. StatPearls / NCBI Bookshelf — Tibial Anterior Compartment Syndrome
**URL:** https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK518970/
**Credibility:** Continuously updated clinical reference reviewed by licensed physicians, published through NCBI/NLM.

Vigorous repetitive exercise and rapid tibialis hypertrophy are known causes of chronic exertional compartment syndrome (CECS). Recommends **gradual progression and appropriate conditioning**. Supports conservative weight increments (2.5 kg) and including a warmup before loading.

---

### 6. ATG Online Coaching — Ben Patrick (Knees Over Toes)
**URL:** https://www.atgonlinecoaching.com/articles/article-atg-shin-ability-exercise-routine
**Credibility:** Not peer-reviewed. Ben Patrick is the inventor of the tib bar and the most widely cited practical authority on tibialis raise programming. His standards are used across the fitness community.

- **Beginner protocol:** 2×20 reps
- **Intermediate standard:** 20% of bodyweight × 20 reps
- **Advanced standard ("tibialis nirvana"):** 25% of bodyweight × 5×5

The advanced standard (5×5 at 25% BW) is notably heavy and low-rep — consistent with the fiber-type evidence. ATG's benchmark is **20 reps**, not 25.

---

### 7. Spencer Locati, PT, DPT — Therapeutic Associates
**URL:** https://www.therapeuticassociates.com/strengthening-anterior-tibialis-tips-exercises/
**Credibility:** Licensed Doctor of Physical Therapy writing for a multi-location PT practice.

Recommends **3 sets to fatigue**. Standard PT practice includes a light unloaded warmup set before adding load. Dosing should be individualized based on clinical examination.

---

### 8. StrengthLog — Andreas Abelsson (Nov 2025)
**URL:** https://www.strengthlog.com/tibialis-anterior-exercises-workout/
**Credibility:** Evidence-based fitness publication; not peer-reviewed but coach/sports-scientist backed.

Recommends **2–3×/week**, placed at the end of leg or calf workouts. Progression model: bodyweight → banded → tib bar → kettlebell.

## Assessment

| Parameter | Original Program | Evidence Recommendation | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sets | 3 | 2–3 | ✅ Optimal |
| Rep range | 15–25 | 15–20 (ATG benchmark: 20) | ⚠️ Upper end too wide |
| Progression | +1 rep/session, +2.5 kg at 3×25 | Sound; 2.5 kg increment reasonable | ✅ Sound, minor tweak |
| Starting weight | 5 kg | Individualized; 5 kg reasonable | ✅ Appropriate |
| Frequency | 2–3×/week | 2–3×/week across all sources | ✅ Optimal |
| Warmup | None | 1×15 BW reps before loading recommended | ⚠️ Gap |

---

## Recommendations & Changes Made

1. **Narrowed rep window to 15–20** (was 15–25). ATG's benchmark is 20 reps; this also hits the weight increase faster and keeps the stimulus in a more strength-adjacent zone given the muscle's fast-twitch fiber composition.

2. **Added bodyweight warmup set** (`1×15 @ 0 kg`). Supported by the DPT source and StatPearls' guidance on gradual conditioning before load.

3. **Long-term recommendation (not yet programmed):** Once working weight exceeds ~15% of bodyweight, consider periodically running 3×8–12 at heavier loads. ATG's advanced standard (25% BW × 5×5) reflects the muscle's fast-twitch nature and is a logical long-term goal.

Tibialis Raise / 3x15 5kg / warmup: 1x15 0kg / progress: dp(2.5kg, 15, 20)
